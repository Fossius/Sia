Sia
Decentralized Storage
Sia is a new decentralized cloud storage platform that gives you control of your data. Before ever being uploaded, your data is split into hundreds of pieces and each piece is encrypted. Then, your data is uploaded to dozens of hosts on the network. A blockchain is used to create cryptographic contracts with each service, ensuring that they only get paid if they actually store your data. Out of dozens of hosts, only 1/3 need to remain online for you to recover the data. Even if many of the hosts are unreliable, your files are still safe.

Anybody can join as a host and make money. This openness allows Sia to take advantage of all the unused storage available in the world, estimated to be millions upon millions of terabytes. This creates a great opportunity for cheap storage. Reliability is maintained by using redundancy and cryptography.

Sia's decentralized approach to cloud storage results in a global network of computers that can be leveraged to improve upon price, efficiency, security, reliability, latency, and throughput over traditional cloud storage. Many of these advantages come from leveraging the network to create advanced algorithms for storing and retrieving data. Many advantages also result from directly correlating storage and bandwidth with income. We saw a similiar phenomenon with Bitcoin mining, where hashrate is very tightly coorelated to income. There is no marketing or customer service overhead for Bitcoin mining. The result was that Bitcoin mining became absurdly efficient, today Bitcoin does more computation than the top 500 supercomputers combined. Sia brings the same direct coorelation between profit and efficient infrastructure. You plug in your hard drives and start making money, with no marketing or customer service overhead. We believe that this, combined with intelligent algorithms for levaraging such a network, will create the best cloud storage platform the world has ever seen.
