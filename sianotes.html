What are Sianotes?
Sianotes are a temporary asset that are convertible to Siafunds. The deadline for conversion is June 1st, 2015. One sianote converts to one siafund. More about Siafunds â†’

Where can I get Sianotes?
Sianotes are available on the Nxt Asset Exchange, and must be purchased at the market price using Nxt.

How can I redeem my Sianotes?
Siafunds will be distributed to their owners in the genesis block. If you have Sianotes, you can convert each Sianote into a Siafund. To claim your Siafunds, you need a Sia address. Download this tool to generate an address:
  • Linux 64bit
  • OS X 64bit
  • Windows 64bit
  • Other Platforms
Once you have an address, send your Sianotes on Nxt to our original address NXT-K5ZJ-NNR2-4J4B-D92UF Include a message that says 'I am redeeming my Sianotes, my Sia address is: [address].' When the Sia network launches, you will have Siafunds at the address provided. The deadline for redemption is June 1st, 2015. The network launches on June 7th, 2015.

FAQ:

Q: What does a sia address look like?
A: A sia address is 64 hexadecimal characters. An example: fbe93b8cdb8ce2496cf1dec11bd9d2ee82b4626a5c8088ecd5c9fe91ac1c3bdd

Q: Do I need to keep the address?
A: You need to keep the key files, typically of the form 'SiafundAddress_KeyN.siakey'. The address can be generated from this keyfile.

Q: Does the message I send on nxt need to follow an exact format?
A: No, we are manually checking all of the messages that we receive. It is okay if you deviate from the format slightly.

Q: Does my message need to be encrypted?
A: Encryption helps your privacy, and it is preferred that you encrypt the message. Encryption is not mandatory however. All that matters is that we can read the message.

Q: Who owns 8780 siafunds?
A: Nebulous, the parent company of Sia development.

Q: What will happen to the unclaimed siafunds?
A: Any siafunds not claimed by June 1st become property of Nebulous, and will join the other 8780. This is a hard deadline for legal reasons. We will most likely honor any late redemptions of sianotes, however we have no legal obligation to do so.
